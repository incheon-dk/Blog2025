Abraham Lincoln’s Gettysburg Address has 272 words.

It can be delivered in 2 or 3 minutes, depending on the speaker.

It is considered one of the most powerful speeches in American history, known for its clarity, moral conviction and rhetorical impact, according to AI summary.

Despite its brevity and maybe because of it, the meaning and purpose and our role in democracy is forcefully taught and remembered.

While cleaning out some drawers, found a set of cassettes that I have owned since the 1980s.

한국가곡 (Korean art songs) are a genre of vocal music that blend Western classical music traditions with Korean language, poetry, and sentiment. They are the Korean counterpart to Western Lieder (German art songs) or chansons (French art songs), and hold a distinct place in Korean music history.

Key Characteristics of 한국가곡:

Lyrics in Korean
Unlike Western lieder (like Schubert or Schumann), Korean art songs are sung in standard Korean, often using poetry by modern Korean poets or texts that evoke traditional Korean values and imagery.

Western Musical Form
The structure, harmony, and vocal techniques are borrowed from Western classical music, particularly the German lied and French mélodie traditions.

Emphasis on Emotion (정서, jeong-seo)
Themes often revolve around longing, nature, love, nostalgia, and han (한) — the uniquely Korean feeling of deep sorrow or unresolved grief.

Piano Accompaniment
Most Korean art songs are composed for a solo voice with piano, similar to Western art songs.

Performance Tradition

These songs are frequently performed in recitals, competitions, and school curriculums for vocal students in Korea.
